EQ Bank Co-op Work Term Report

Hamza Memon · Summer 2025 · University of Guelph

Introduction

During the Summer 2025 semester, I completed a four month co-op work term at EQ Bank (Equitable Bank) in Toronto. My role was with the Retail Lending Technology team, where I contributed to projects involving API integration, system improvements, and documentation. This opportunity allowed me to apply classroom concepts in a professional fintech setting while gaining valuable exposure to enterprise-scale workflows.

About the Employer

EQ Bank is Canada’s leading digital only bank, offering innovative products such as high interest savings accounts, mortgages, and lending services. A division of Equitable Bank, EQ Bank focuses heavily on technology driven solutions to provide modern financial services to Canadians. Within this environment, the Retail Lending Technology team supports and enhances the systems powering EQ Bank’s lending products, giving me direct exposure to how computing supports financial innovation.

Goals & Learning Outcomes

At the start of my co-op, I set out several goals aligned with both my academic growth and job responsibilities. These included strengthening my technical foundation, gaining exposure to enterprise technologies, and improving professional communication. My goals were directly related to my job tasks, which required programming, API integration, and collaboration with teammates in an agile environment.

I wanted to build skills in debugging, writing cleaner code, and using version control effectively, all of which will benefit my next work experience by making me a more efficient contributor to large codebases. I also aimed to explore technologies such as Java, Spring Boot, Docker, Angular, and CI/CD pipelines, since they are widely used in enterprise environments and will be valuable in future co-op terms and career opportunities.

Reflecting on these goals, I am proud to say I completed the majority of them. I successfully strengthened my technical expertise, became more comfortable with industry tools, and improved my ability to communicate technical concepts clearly. While I met my learning goals, one area I would have liked more hands on experience in was frontend frameworks like Angular. Although I gained exposure through shadowing and documentation, I did not contribute as deeply to that area as I did with backend technologies.

Learning Goal 1: Critical & Creative Thinking – Depth & Breadth of Understanding

Goal: Apply object-oriented programming (OOP) concepts and debugging strategies in a professional codebase.

Action Plan:

  • Review and analyze my team’s existing codebase for opportunities to apply OOP and debugging techniques.
  • Use Git for version control (branching, merging, conflict resolution).
  • Participate in weekly code reviews, giving and receiving constructive feedback.

Measure of Success:

  • Demonstrated improvement in clarity and quality of code review comments.
  • Successfully resolved merge conflicts and committed clean code following best practices.
  • Positive feedback from senior developers on my understanding of OOP concepts.
Learning Goal 2: Critical & Creative Thinking – Breadth of Technical Knowledge

Goal: Become familiar with technologies such as Java, Spring Boot, Angular, Docker, and CI/CD pipelines by observing and documenting real-world usage.

Action Plan:

  • Shadow teammates and review project repositories to understand how each tool fits into workflows.
  • Explore official documentation and tutorials for each framework.
  • Document the purpose and implementation details of tools used by the team.

Measure of Success:

  • Produced a clear document outlining usage of each technology.
  • Demonstrated ability to contribute to tasks involving these frameworks.
  • Received positive feedback from supervisors on initiative and technical growth.
Learning Goal 3: Communication – Integrative Professional Communication

Goal: Improve professional communication through active meeting participation and structured documentation.

Action Plan:

  • Prepare discussion points/questions before team meetings.
  • Contribute to technical discussions during stand-ups and sprint reviews.
  • Document work in a structured format using team tools (e.g., Confluence, README files).

Measure of Success:

  • Feedback from team members highlighting strong meeting contributions.
  • Well-written documentation accepted and used by the team.
  • Improved confidence when explaining technical concepts to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Job Description

My role centered on software development and integration tasks within the Retail Lending Technology team. Responsibilities included:

  • Assisting with API workflows that supported secure communication between services.
  • Supporting testing and troubleshooting using tools such as Postman and Kibana.
  • Creating step by step technical documentation to support onboarding and knowledge transfer.
  • Actively participating in Agile ceremonies (daily stand-ups, sprint planning, reviews).

This position required knowledge of programming, debugging, and software engineering principles learned in class, while also pushing me to adapt to enterprise grade security and system requirements.

Conclusions

This work term was a transformative experience. It reinforced the importance of:

  • Balancing technical depth with clear communication.
  • Writing secure, scalable, and maintainable code.
  • Understanding how computing systems directly support financial services in a regulated industry.

Overall, I left this role with stronger technical, problem solving, and teamwork skills, which will serve me in future work terms and my career.
